Title : AI-Powered Smart Energy Consumption Prediction System

Abstract : Accurate prediction of energy consumption is essential for improving energy efficiency,
optimizing resource utilization, and ensuring sustainability in smart city environments.
With the rapid growth of smart buildings, IoT-enabled devices, and intelligent energy
systems, effective energy demand forecasting has become increasingly important for
reducing operational costs, minimizing energy wastage, and enabling better decisionmaking in energy management. The motivation behind this study is to develop an intelligent and reliable energy consumption prediction system that can accurately estimate future energy usage while providing insights into the key factors influencing consumption. To achieve this, a comprehensive methodology combining machine learning
and deep learning techniques is proposed using real-world smart city sensor data, including air conditioning load, lighting load, plug load, temperature, humidity, ambient
light, and temporal features such as year, month, day, and hour. Machine learning
models such as Ridge Regression, K-Nearest Neighbors (KNN), Decision Tree, Random Forest, and XGBoost, along with deep learning models including Artificial Neural
Networks (ANN), LSTM, and a Hybrid Transformer–LSTM model, are implemented
and evaluated. Experimental results demonstrate that among all the models, LSTM
emerged as the best-performing model, effectively capturing complex temporal dependencies and energy usage patterns, while the Hybrid Transformer–LSTM model also
contributed to improved predictive analysis. Ensemble models such as Random Forest
and XGBoost delivered strong performance, further validating the effectiveness of the
proposed framework. Additionally, SHAP (SHapley Additive exPlanations) analysis
was integrated to enhance model interpretability, identifying temporal features, plug
load, and air-conditioning load as the most influential factors affecting energy consumption. The proposed system provides accurate, reliable, and explainable energy
forecasts, supporting efficient load balancing, intelligent energy optimization, and sustainable smart energy management in smart city infrastructures.
